The problem

You know when it's off. You just can't say why.

You've written things that sounded exactly right — direct, warm, distinctly you. Then, under deadline, something slips. Voice drift is quiet. You rarely catch it in the moment.

01

You feel the drift — but you can't name it.

A sentence reads wrong. Something's too formal, too flat, too safe. You rewrite it three times and you're still not sure you got there. There's no benchmark. Just instinct.

02

You hire a writer. They sound like every other writer.

They're competent. They hit the brief. But what they produce sounds like content, not you. Now you spend more time editing than you would have just writing it yourself.

03

You use AI to move faster. Now everything sounds the same.

Your newsletter reads like a terms-of-service document that attended a TED talk. The ideas are yours. The voice isn't. And your readers can tell.

The Signal Engine

Six steps. One standard. Yours.

Every review runs on The Signal Engine — a six-step writing framework built to identify what makes your voice distinct, then measure everything against it. Not generic AI tone-checking. A real methodology.

01

The Scene-Set

Does your opening pull people in or ease them in? There's a difference — and only one of them works.

02

Aiism Eliminator

Catches every pattern that makes writing sound like it was generated, not thought. If a machine would write it, you shouldn't.

03

7 Tools of Trust

Vagueness kills credibility. Flags every claim that isn't backed by specificity — the kind readers feel before they can name it.

04

The Pivot

When your writing needs to move from story to point, does it land cleanly or lurch awkwardly? The pivot is where most drafts lose their readers.

05

The Loop Strategy

Open a question early. Answer it late. Does your structure pull readers forward, or give them an easy exit every paragraph?

06

Angles of Attraction

Is your content saying something, or just occupying space? Find the angle your competitors avoided and write from there.
